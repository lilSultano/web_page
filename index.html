After Nice, Berlin, and Barcelona, cities installed barriers. Billions were spent. But no city has ever verified whether its defenses would actually stop an attack. That is the gap IGUDAR closes.

01

THE PLATFORM

Every city has blind spots.
IGUDAR finds them.

A piazza with three bollards on the main road. A side street, 12 meters wide, with no protection at all.

Cities defend the obvious routes. Attackers don't use obvious routes. IGUDAR maps every approach corridor — including the ones that appear irrelevant — because the vulnerability that matters is always the one no one assessed.

A security consultant places barriers based on experience. But experience doesn't account for 200 possible approach vectors.

IGUDAR simulates thousands of attack scenarios with real vehicle physics — mass, velocity, turning radius. It identifies the gaps that human assessment structurally cannot see, and recommends placements that are mathematically optimal.

A city spends €200,000 on barriers. The mayor holds a press conference. Everyone feels safer. But no one has ever tried to break through them.

IGUDAR's adversarial AI attacks the city's own defenses — probing, adapting, exploiting every gap. If the defense fails, it fails in simulation, generating actionable data. Not in the field, generating casualties.

02

HOW IT WORKS

From raw city data
to validated defense.

01

Ingest

IGUDAR loads real-world geographic data and builds a precise digital twin of the urban environment at sub-meter resolution. Every street, every curb, every infrastructure element.

02

Analyze

The platform identifies every corridor a vehicle could use to reach the target zone. Not just the main roads — every side street, every passage, every overlooked vulnerability in the perimeter.

03

Defend

An AI agent places barriers in the optimal configuration, adapting to the evolving tactical landscape as corridors close. The objective function is single-minded: minimize projected casualties to zero.

04

Validate

A Red Agent — adversarial AI — attempts to breach the defense. If it succeeds, the configuration is revised. You receive a tactical report with casualty projections, velocity analysis, and regulatory compliance.

OUR VISION

Defense through
geometry.

IGUDAR takes its name from the igudar — the fortified granaries of the Amazigh people in southern Morocco. Built into mountainsides, they protected entire communities for centuries. Not with armies, but with the geometry of the environment itself.

Our mission follows the same principle: use the shape of a city as its own defense. An adversarial optimization engine that adapts to any scenario — from a market square to a commercial port, from a stadium to a government district.
